    The Candidates
    Democrats won big in The White House, Arizona and Colorado, but will need to win both Georgia runoff races to flip the Senate. Runoff elections are second elections that happen when no candidate wins 50% of the vote. The elections will be held in Georgia on January 5, 2021.

    Why It’s Important to Donate:

    Donating makes the party less reliant on big donors and raises awareness about the candidates. Let’s do our part and contribute.
    It’s Not Peachy Yet
    We have good news and bad news. If election day (week) proved anything, it’s that Georgia is no longer a reliably red state. The Georgia tide is shifting from red to purple thanks to the quickly expanding Atlanta metro area and the hard work of people like Stacey Abrams to register hundreds of thousands of voters across the state.

    The bad news? Georgia has not elected a Democratic senator in 20 years, meaning we have our work cut out for us. Plus, in the recent election Georgians waited as long as 11 hours to vote in some precincts and predominantly Black precincts in the state were more likely to have the longest wait times, despite a surge in voter registrations in those communities.

    Jon Ossoff
    Ossoff is an investigative journalist, business owner, and Georgia native who cares about healthcare, clean energy, small businesses, and rooting out corruption in the government.

    Opponent: David Perdue (Former CEO of Reebok)

    The Scoop:
    • Senator Perdue, who has been in office since 2014, got more votes than Ossoff in the November 3 election, but it wasn’t enough to prevent a runoff. This election isn’t going to be an easy win.
    • Ossoff has focused much of his campaign on health care (prescription drug prices and the government’s handling of the pandemic), and on Perdue’s closeness to Trump.
    • Perdue was expected to have a smooth road to victory, but was weighed down by voters’ displeasure with Trump’s coronavirus response and his own ads and remarks about Ossoff and Harris, which were accused of being anti-Semetic and racially insensitive.

    Reverend Raphael Warnock
    Warnock is a progressive pastor of the Ebenezer Baptist Church, the former pulpit of Reverend Dr. Martin Luther King, Jr. and where Representative John Lewis, the civil-rights icon, was laid to rest. If he wins, he would be the first Black senator from Georgia, ever.

    Opponent: Kelly Loeffler (Her WNBA team would rather Vote Warnock)

    The Scoop:
    • Warnock won the most votes against his two Republican opponents (Loeffler and Collins), but not enough to secure 50 percent of the vote.
    • Warnock’s campaign will continue to focus on expanding access to health care and protecting voting rights.
    • During a Savannah campaign stop in August, Loeffler said she was “the only U.S. Senator that has 100% Senate voting record with our president,” and that she was out to help him “drain the swamp”
