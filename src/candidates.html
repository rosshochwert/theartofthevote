    The Candidates
    There are 35 senate battles happening, but some races are more competitive than others. We highlighted the most important races that need your support to give Democrats the best chances of gaining three to four seats. Read up on each candidate and choose your battle, or split your donation across all candidates by going to the Full Team page. And if you donate $20 or more, go to the Claim Your Swag page for instructions on how to have your sticker pack delivered.

    Why It’s Important to Donate:

    Donating makes the party less reliant on big donors and raises awareness about the candidates. Let’s do our part and contribute.
    Big Four
    Put your hands together for our best pick up opportunities.


    Arizona: Mark Kelly
    Kelly is a retired US Navy combat pilot, engineer and NASA astronaut — no biggie. He cares about health care, economy, gun safety and fixing the broken immigration system.

    Opponent: Martha McSally (Was appointed to fill the seat of the late Senator John McCain, so she hasn’t actually won an election)

    Colorado: John Hickenlooper
    Colorado has been reliably blue since 2014, so we have a good chance here. Hickenlooper is a former governor and ran for president in 2020. He cares about small businesses, gun safety and the environment. Fun fact — he opened the first brewpub in Colorado.

    Opponent: Senator Cory Gardner (Reliably votes with Trump and the Republicans)

    Learn More About Hickenlooper
    DONATE NOW


    Maine: Sara Gideon
    Despite Maine going for Hillary in 2016 and the Democrats flipping the congressional district in 2018, it’s still dicey. Gideon is currently Maine Speaker of the House and cares about healthcare, the economy and education.

    Opponent: Senator Susan Collins (AKA the world’s most unpopular Senator)

    Learn More About Gideon
    DONATE NOW


    North Carolina: Cal Cunningham
    North Carolina is considered another swing state. Cunningham is a former senator, an Afghanistan and Iraq war veteran, and helped form an environmental waste reduction company. He cares about education, environment and campaign finance reform, and was Vice Chairman of the Governor Roy Cooper’s Crime Commission.

    Opponent: Senator Thom Tillis (Names his dogs after Republican presidents)

    Learn More About Cunningham

    Toss-Ups
    These states traditionally lean red, but have been surprisingly close races recently. They’re essential to cement our four-seat gain.


    Georgia: Jon Ossoff
    This is a highly competitive face off, heavily funded by both Democrats and Republicans. Georgia has not elected a Democratic senator in 20 years but the state is shifting from red to purple. Ossoff is an investigative journalist, business owner, and Georgia native who cares about healthcare, clean energy, small businesses, and rooting out corruption in the government.

    Opponent: David Perdue (Former CEO of Reebok)

    Learn More About Ossoff
    DONATE NOW


    Iowa: Theresa Greenfield
    Greenfield is an urban planner and real estate developer who cares about defending Social Security and Medicare. That focus might seem a little retro, but if she wins her campaign may inform how Democrats can regain ground from Republicans in rural America.

    Opponent: Joni Ernst(Of the Make Em Squeal Campaign...what?!)

    Learn More About Greenfield
    DONATE NOW


    Montana: Steve Bullock
    Montana doesn’t register voters by party and has a proud history of political individualism. Bullock is a popular two-term Montana governor, former attorney general, and briefly ran for president in 2020. He’s credited with keeping infection rates relatively low and cares about healthcare, the economy and public lands.

    Opponent: Steve Daines (Another Trump lover)

    Defend
    These Democratic seats are not in solidly blue states and are the most at risk to flip.


    Alabama: Doug Jones
    Trump’s net approval rating is very high in Alabama, and Jones voted to convict Trump on both impeachment counts. When Jones was a US Attorney he prosecuted the Klansmen responsible for the infamous 1963 bombing of the 16th Street Baptist Church, bringing justice to the four young girls who died. He is a proponent for affordable health care, civil rights, education and the economy.

    Opponent: Tommy Tuberville (The shady former Auburn University football coach)

    Learn More About Jones
    DONATE NOW


    Michigan: Gary Peters
    Michigan is also considered a swing state. Although Trump won in 2016, it was by his narrowest margin and Michigan elected Democratic Governor Gretchen Whitmer in 2018. Peters was a long-serving House member before he was senator, and has been recognized as bipartisan and an effective lawmaker in the Senate.

    Opponent: John James (Conservative media darling)

    Learn More About Peters
    DONATE NOW


    Reach
    These races might be a stretch, but could flip under the right conditions. Besides, how sweet a victory would it be to unseat McConnell the grim reaper himself?


    Alaska: Dr. Al Gross
    Alaska features the highest number of independent voters of any state. Gross is an independent candidate running for the Democratic party. He’s an orthopedic surgeon and commercial fisherman who cares about public health insurance and renewable energy.

    Opponent: Dan Sullivan (He’s not even from Alaska!)

    Learn More About Gross
    DONATE NOW


    Georgia (Special Election): Rev. Raphael Warnock
    Candidates from all parties will compete on the same ballot, but if no one earns a majority of the vote the special election will resume with a January 5 runoff between the top two candidates. Warnock is a progressive pastor of the Ebenezer Baptist Church, the former pulpit of Reverend Dr. Martin Luther King, Jr. and where Representative John Lewis, the civil-rights icon, was laid to rest. If he wins, he would be the first Black senator from Georgia, ever.

    Opponent 1: Kelly Loeffler (Her WNBA team would rather Vote Warnock)
    Opponent 2: Representative Doug Collins(Supports guns and opposes abortion)

    Learn More About Warnock
    DONATE NOW


    Kansas: Dr. Barbara Bollier
    Dr. Bollier is a physician and former moderate Republican. She served in the Kansas House of Representatives and is currently a Kansas State Senator. If she wins she’d be the first Democratic senator in the state since 1939 — the party’s longest losing streak. She’s campaigning as a voice of moderation, but supports healthcare, ethics reform and seniors.

    Opponent: Senator Roger Marshall (Also a doctor, but uses hydroxychloroquine...)

    Learn More About Bollier
    DONATE NOW


    Kentucky: Amy McGrath
    This race is particularly symbolic (#DitchMitch anyone?) and one of the most expensive Senate races in 2020. McGrath is giving McConnell a literal run for his money, raking in millions and forcing him to step up his game. McGrath is a former Marine fighter pilot and the first woman in the Corps to fly a combat mission in an F/A-18. She served as a Congressional Fellow and a Marine Corps liaison in the Pentagon.

    Opponent: Senator Mitch McConnell (nope, just no)

    Learn More About McGrath
    DONATE NOW


    South Carolina: Jaime Harrison
    No Democrat has won a Senate race in S.C. since 1998, but in recent polls, Harrison is close behind—even tied with—Senator Graham. Harrison is a former state senator, former chairman of the S.C. Democratic Party, and was aide to legendary Congressman James Clyburn. Much of his career has been devoted to enfranchising Black voters, and he cares deeply about affordable and accessible healthcare.

    Opponent: Lindsey Graham (Check out his Kavanaugh speech)

    Learn More About Harrison
    DONATE NOW


    Texas: M.J. Hegar
    Texas has been a Republican bastion for decades, but Democrats have gained ground. Hegar gained national attention in 2018 for a powerful ad highlighting her work breaking down barriers for women in the military. A combat veteran and search and rescue pilot in the US Air Force, Hegar received a Purple Heart for injuries she sustained in a search and rescue mission.

    Opponent: John Cornyn (Former Senate Majority Whip)
