    Why You Should Care
    During Trump’s presidency, the Senate has experienced setback after setback. The Senate is the reason Brett Kavanaugh is on the Supreme Court and the Violence Against Women Act wasn’t reauthorized. Meanwhile, the Democratic-led House has approved hundreds of measures since January 2019, only to be halted in the Senate. They passed the Heroes Act package for COVID-19 relief, impeached Trump and passed an immigration bill to protect “dreamers.” But none of these measures made a difference in Mitch McConnell’s Senate.

    Think of what we could accomplish with a Democratic-led Senate:
    • Mandate a $15 minimum wage (Did you know the federal mandate is $7.25 and has not been changed since 2009?)
    • Reallocate police budgets
    • Ensure every American citizen has access to a free coronavirus vaccine, when it is available
    • Recommit to the WHO and Paris Climate Agreement
    • Act to combat climate change
    No candidate or party is perfect, but who else is ready to take a left?
    How We Do It
    Senators are reelected every six years, and elections are staggered so 1/3 of the Senate is up for reelection every even calendar year. That means in 2020, Republicans are defending 23 seats and Democrats are defending 12 seats. To halt — and repair — the damage caused by McConnell’s Senate, your vote counts more than ever.

    Fast Facts:
    • The Senate currently has 53 Republicans and 47 Democrats (If we include the two Independents).
    • All the seats Democrats are defending in 2020 are in solidly blue states, except for two.
    • Democrats need to gain four seats in November’s election or they need to gain three and win back the White House, which would allow the vice president to cast tie breaking votes.
